Scraps

Here is my final project within the full-stack bootcamp where we were encouraged to use our newfound knowledge of React. The theme of the website is supposed to give the user that nostalgic feeling of surfing the web in the 90s. It is centered around the Spotify API where users can simply look up a song through mood, title, album name, or lyrics. Once the desired song is found they can then preview the song. Designer's Delve

This was my second group project where my group was able to incorporate back-end functionalities into our website such as a server and a database. As a group we wanted to build a site that attracted artists and people who wanted to view art. Our landing page welcomes the user with animated artwork and shortly sends them to our homepage. Once the user is on the homepage they get two simple options: View the artists work or if your an artist post your work to the website. The post option requires you to fill out a form to add information to your art so other users know who you are, what type of art your showcasing, and a description of the art piece. The view option allows users to cycle through the different art such as photography, illustrations, etc. and gain information about the artist through what information they chose to fill out.

Movie List (Practice Project)

I made this project to simply practice and showcase the skills I learned after graduating the bootcamp I attended. The application only has two pages; on the homepage there is a form to fill out for any movie you desire. Once filled out you click submit and it will add it to the database which will then be displayed on the right side of the screen. Then from there you click on the movie you added or any movie that is listed which will take you to the second page. This page is for displaying details about the selected movie using an API call to IMDB. I wanted to design a simple full-stack application with a front end and back end allowing me to get more comfortable doing projects on my own.
